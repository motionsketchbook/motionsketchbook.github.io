About me

Neville Brody(born 23 April 1957) is an English graphic designer, typographer and art director.[1] He is known for his work on The Face magazine (1981–1986), Arena magazine (1987–1990), and designing record covers for artists such as Clock DVA, Cabaret Voltaire, The Bongos, 23 Skidoo and Depeche Mode. He created the company Research Studios in 1994 and is a founding member of Fontworks. His work is included in the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA).[2] He was the Dean of the School of Communication at the Royal College of Art, London until September 2018. He is now Professor of Communication.
